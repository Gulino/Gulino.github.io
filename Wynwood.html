Wynwood

One neighborhood, thousands of colors

I have been several times in Wynwood and every time I discover a new gallery, restaurant or another amazing graffiti art work! Yes, Wynwood, together with the Design District is one of the most well-known art district of a city, whose cultural art scene is booming. Wynwood is a very young neighborhood, and only two decades ago was a really different place. It was originally a Puerto Rican, called El Barrio or San Juan working class neighbourhood, mostly known for its big warehouses and factories. The first step towards its transformation was in 1987: a group of artists purchased the former Flowers Baking Company building transforming it in Florida’s largest working artists’ space, Bakehouse Art Complex, which it’s still working today. Since then, artists began to move to Wynwood, thanks to its big empty spaces and its affordable rents, and in 2000’s the neighborhood experienced a great attention in terms of real estate investments. Studio artists, galleries, lounges, restaurants, clubs began to open transforming Wynwood in the most vibrant neighborhood in Great Miami!

Wynwood Walls

The Wynwood Walls started in 2009, by one of the key man of Wynwood’s success: Tony Goldman, a developer who wanted to transform the neighborhood in the biggest open-air graffiti art museum in the world. Comprised of six separate warehouses, the walls are giant canvases for the greatest graffiti artists, more than 90 since the project was born.

Art in Wynwood

If you are an art lover and you are visiting Wynwood, don’t miss the Rubell Family Collection, one of the largest, privately owned, publicly accessible contemporary art collections in the world. The collection is exhibited in Wynwood since 1993, within a Drug Enforcement Agency confiscated building, and every year there are different thematic exhibitions. If you love art, but also food, music, and parties, watch out the Wynwood Art Walk! Every second Saturday of the month Wynwood’s galleries, boutiques and restaurants are open after hours to the public, creating an exciting block party with live music and food trucks all night long!
